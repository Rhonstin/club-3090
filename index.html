Research findings

What we learned

Non-obvious results from ~15 models across hermesagent-20 and the full 8-pack suite.

01
Single-pack benchmarks are selection bias
Carnice I-Quality scores 85% on hermesagent-20 alone — looks dominant. Add CLI-40 and it drops to 63.5% overall, ranking #7 of 8. The model that looks worst on hermesagent (byteshape, 60%) wins the full suite. Always test across the full workload surface.
02
Fine-tune quality >> base model size
APEX-I-Quality 35B-A3B (base weights before Carnice fine-tuning): 11/20 (55%) on hermesagent. Carnice on the identical base: 17/20 (85%). That's +30 percentage points from fine-tuning alone. Quant depth, MTP, bigger context — none of it helps as much as the right fine-tune.
03
Coder models fail agent tasks
Qwen3-Coder-30B-A3B scored 9/20 (45%) — the worst result in the entire dataset. At 170 TPS it's the fastest model tested. It failed HA-10 (skill discovery), which every other model passes. The coder fine-tune overwrote general instruction-following with code-specific patterns.
04
CLI-40 is the great equalizer
Largest spread of any pack: 6.7 to 20.0 out of 40 — a 3× range. Models fine-tuned narrowly (Carnice, GLM) collapse here. Models with broad general training (byteshape base, Qwopus) dominate. If you pick a model for DevOps/automation, CLI-40 is more predictive than any agent-protocol pack.
05
Thinking ON hurts agent scores (with DFlash)
beellama/DFlash with thinking ON: 12/20 (60%). Same model, thinking OFF: 14/20 (70%). The DFlash speculative decoder suppresses speculation on tool-call markers — thinking tokens trigger this more often, giving slower generation and worse agent behavior simultaneously.
06
All 35B-A3B base quants plateau at 60%
Tested Q4_K_M (140 TPS), IQ4_XS + MTP (115 TPS), UD-Q4_K_M + MTP n=4 (99 TPS). They all hit 12–13/20 (60–65%) on hermesagent. Throw MTP, better quant, larger context at it — doesn't move the number. The base model is the ceiling; only fine-tuning breaks it.
07
OOM is silent and catastrophic
Carnice-V2 first run: q8_0 KV + 120K ctx = 20 MB VRAM free. Model appeared to load and respond normally. Score: 3/20. Multi-turn hermes conversations silently exhausted KV cache mid-session. Fix: q4_0 KV + 65K ctx → 3.3 GB free → 13/20. Always check headroom, not just "did it boot".
08
Safety clarification (HA-20) — only 2 models
The scenario: an ambiguous "delete X" request. Correct behavior is to ask for clarification before acting. Only Carnice I-Quality and Deck-Opus 40B pass this. Every other model executes immediately. If you're building anything where destructive actions need confirmation — this is your filter.
